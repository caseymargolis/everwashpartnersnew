### Start with Preventive Maintenance

The best defense against winter problems is a proactive one. Schedule equipment inspections before the first major freeze and make sure every component is winter-ready.

Key areas to inspect:

* Pipes and Valves: Insulate all exposed lines and check for leaks or weak joints.
* Hydraulics and Air Compressors: Drain water from tanks and lines daily to prevent freezing.
* Bay Doors and Seals: Repair worn weather stripping and make sure doors close fully to retain heat.
* Floors and Drains: Keep them clear to prevent ice buildup that can endanger both customers and staff.

Use the EverWash Partner Portal to document maintenance routines, schedule recurring inspections, or keep digital notes for your team — ensuring accountability and easy access to operational records.

### Stay Operational with Smart Heating Practices

Even small heating improvements can make a big difference in uptime and safety.

* Maintain ambient bay temperatures: Use heaters or radiant floor systems to keep critical components above freezing.
* Install windbreaks or curtains: They help retain heat and prevent overspray from turning into ice.
* Check equipment shelters: Verify that water reclaim systems, vacuums, and pay stations have adequate insulation and heat tracing.

A few proactive upgrades now can save you from costly repairs or downtime later.
